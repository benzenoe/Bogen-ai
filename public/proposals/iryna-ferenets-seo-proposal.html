The Diagnosis

Why Your SEO Isn't Working

This isn't an effort problem — it's an architecture problem. Three structural issues are holding your visibility down, and all three trace back to one root cause: you don't own your website.

1

You're renting your content

The blog and pages you've paid Luxury Presence to produce are locked inside a platform you don't own. Leave, and you leave them behind — every post, every URL, every ranking signal. You are paying, month after month, to improve an asset that will never be yours.

2

Your current site is nearly invisible to AI

Luxury Presence sites are heavy JavaScript applications. Our audit measured your homepage at 8.5 MB across 117 requests — including 5.6 MB of JavaScript that builds your content in the browser. AI crawlers (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot) and modern search systems favor fast, clean, readable HTML. If an AI engine can't cleanly read your page, it cannot cite you in its answer.

3

You can't touch the levers that matter

On a rented site you can't fully control structured-data markup, the robots.txt file, page speed, or how content is organized for AI extraction. Those are the AEO levers. The platform that makes you unrankable is also the one selling you SEO.

The honest conclusion

You can do real, lasting SEO and AI-search optimization — but not on a rented Luxury Presence site. That's why this proposal starts where you are today, then shows you the upgrade that removes the ceiling entirely.

Findings, Ranked by Impact

Critical

No structured data anywhere on your sitePhase 1 + 2

Not one page — homepage, listing pages, or your About page — carries schema markup. Google and AI assistants have no machine-readable way to know you are a New York real estate agent, where you work, or what your listings cost. Structured data is the foundation of AI-search visibility, and it is entirely absent.

Critical

5.6 MB of JavaScript renders your contentPhase 2

Your pages are assembled in the browser by script rather than served as readable HTML. Many AI crawlers do not run JavaScript — so they may see a nearly empty page. The single biggest reason you are hard to find in AI search is that your site is hard for AI to read.

High

No answer-engine strategy in placePhase 1 + 2

Your robots.txt gives no instruction to AI crawlers (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended), your content is not structured as extractable questions and answers, and there is no llms.txt. Nothing about the site is built to earn a citation inside an AI answer.

High

Thin, generic listing-page titlesPhase 1

A live listing titled simply "2241 PLUMB 1ST Street 3M" — 24 characters, no neighborhood, no price, no mention of you. Listing pages are where buyers search; these titles give Google and AI almost nothing to rank or quote.

High

Your blog is orphaned from the rest of the sitePhase 1

You publish a strong neighborhood post almost every week — yet "Blog" appears nowhere in your main navigation and nowhere in your footer. One low-prominence link is the only way in. Visitors never discover the content, and Google passes it almost no internal authority. You are paying for weekly content that the site itself hides.

High

Domain authority near 5/100 — almost no backlinksPhase 1

Your domain authority sits near 5 out of 100. Weekly publishing hasn't moved it, because authority is driven by backlinks — other reputable sites linking to yours — and you have very few. This is the clearest, most fixable gap on the list, and backlink and digital-PR outreach is core Phase 1 work.

Moderate

Inconsistent image alt text, no geo-taggingPhase 1

Nearly half the images on your homepage carry empty or missing alt text, and images are not geo-tagged for local relevance. For a location-driven business, every untagged property and neighborhood image is a missed local-search signal.

Moderate

Slow load time hurts rankings and crawl depthPhase 2

At 3.9 seconds to load, your homepage fails Google's Core Web Vitals "good" threshold. Slow pages rank lower, and crawlers — human or AI — give a heavy site less of their attention.

Working

What's already solid — and worth keeping

To be fair to the fundamentals: your main pages have clear title tags and meta descriptions, a single H1 each, Open Graph tags, correct canonical URLs, and a working sitemap. The on-page basics are competent. The failures above are structural — and structural problems cannot be fixed on a platform you don't own.

The pattern

Some of these — your orphaned blog, thin titles, missing backlinks — we can fix immediately under Phase 1. The deepest ones — schema, speed, AI-readability — are locked by the rented platform and need the Phase 2 rebuild. Either way, this audit doesn't just diagnose the problem; it scopes the work.
