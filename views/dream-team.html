The Principles

Why This Actually Works.

A Dream Team isn't a prompt trick. It's an operating principle. Three things make it work — and they're true whether you're a solo founder or running a 50-person company.

01

Tension Beats Consensus

One advisor gives you one view. Six advisors who disagree give you the truth. The insight isn't in the agreement — it's in the space between conflicting perspectives. A board of yes-men is worse than no board at all.

02

Context is the Multiplier

An advisor with no context is a fortune cookie. An advisor trained on your numbers, offers, goals, and constraints is a $25K/month consultant. The gap isn't in the model — it's in what you feed it. Most people skip this step and then wonder why AI advice feels generic.

03

Speed Changes Decisions

The decisions that kill businesses aren't wrong decisions — they're the ones you keep putting off. When advice is eight minutes away instead of three weeks, you stop procrastinating. You start moving. Compounding clarity is the real unlock.

Unlocked — Real Session

One Real Dream Team Session.

Here's an actual condensed session. Two advisors, one question — so you can see how the conversation flows before you download the full playbook.

The Question
"I'm launching a $997 AI course for Realtors in 30 days. Should I run it as a live cohort or evergreen self-study?"
Seth Godin — The Marketer
Cohort. Every time. Evergreen is a commodity — every creator has an evergreen course. A cohort is an event. It has a beginning, a middle, an end, and a tribe that moves through it together. People don't buy information anymore; they buy belonging. Evergreen sells once and disappears. A cohort becomes a brand.
Warren Buffett — The Capital Allocator
Seth is right about positioning, but consider the economics. Cohort means capped enrollment, fixed delivery hours, and your personal time as the bottleneck. You can charge more per seat, but you're trading scale for premium. If your operating constraint is time, you want evergreen with a cohort tier on top. Build both. Sell the cohort as the premium.

Synthesis

Agreement: Cohorts create belonging. Evergreen alone is weak positioning.

Tension: Godin says "cohort or bust." Buffett says "evergreen for scale, cohort for margin."

The call: Launch with a cohort first to build the brand and case studies. Open evergreen six months later once you have proof. Price cohort at $1,997 (premium), evergreen at $997 (volume).

Two Free Lessons

The Mistakes That Kill Most Dream Teams.

I've seen hundreds of founders try to build their own. Here are the first two mistakes that wreck it — so you can avoid them before you even start.

01

Letting the AI Be Polite

The default behavior of every modern AI is to agree with you. That's poison for an advisor. If your advisors are nodding along with everything you say, your prompts are weak.

The fix: Add one line to every single advisor prompt — "If the user is wrong, tell them. Don't be polite." That single line transforms the entire system from sycophancy to strategy.

02

Asking Fortune-Cookie Questions

"How do I grow?" will get you a generic answer every time. "I need to go from $40K MRR to $80K MRR in 6 months with a team of 2 and a $10K ad budget — what's the sharpest path?" will get you real advice.

The fix: Every Dream Team question needs three ingredients — current state, desired state, and real constraints. The more specific the question, the sharper the advice.
