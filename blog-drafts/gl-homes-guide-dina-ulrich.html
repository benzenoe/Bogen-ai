The GL Homes Story: Nearly 50 Years Building Florida's Finest

GL Homes isn't just another national builder with a Florida office. Founded in 1976 by Itzhak Ezratti and his father-in-law Joseph Hanin, the company started with a single duplex in Hollywood, Florida. Today, they're the largest private homebuilder in Palm Beach County and the 9th largest private homebuilder in the United States.

What sets GL Homes apart is their Florida-exclusive focus. Every home is engineered specifically for our climate—concrete block construction, hurricane-impact windows and doors, and advanced drainage systems. When the Chinese drywall crisis hit years ago, GL Homes replaced all affected materials at their own expense while other builders walked away. That's the kind of company you're dealing with.

The company remains family-owned, with Misha Ezratti (honored as South Florida Business Journal's "Ultimate CEO" in November 2024) now leading operations. Over 100,000 residents currently live in GL Homes communities statewide.

Boca Bridges: The Pinnacle of West Boca Luxury

If there's a flagship GL Homes community, it's Boca Bridges. Located on Lyons Road between Clint Moore and Atlantic Avenue, this 504-home enclave represents the builder's most ambitious achievement in our market.

The Amenity Package

Here's what makes Boca Bridges special: you get country club amenities without the $150,000+ initiation fee. The 27,000-square-foot clubhouse features CUT 5 gourmet restaurant with poolside seating, six lighted clay tennis courts, four pickleball courts, indoor basketball, resort-style pools with beach entry, a full fitness center with spa services, and 24/7 manned security with individual home alarm monitoring included.

HOA fees run $1,120–$1,173 monthly—substantial, but remarkable value for what's included.

Seven Bridges: Established Luxury with Proven Appreciation

Seven Bridges in Delray Beach predates Boca Bridges as GL Homes' first "mega-community" in our area. Spanning 412 acres with 701 single-family homes built from 2015-2019, this community offers the advantage of maturity—established landscaping, a proven track record, and a well-developed social scene.

The Lotus Communities: Contemporary Design Takes Center Stage

GL Homes' Lotus brand represents a strategic pivot to 100% contemporary architecture. If you prefer clean lines, open floor plans, and modern aesthetics over Mediterranean or transitional styles, Lotus is your answer.

Lotus (Original) – The Trendsetter

Built 2019-2020 with 805 homes, the original Lotus established the brand's identity. Current resale pricing runs $1,300,000–$3,300,000, with an average of $2,015,070 and price per square foot averaging $389 (up to $720 for premium lakefront). The 94% list-to-sale ratio tells you buyers are motivated.

Lotus Palm – Enhanced Finishes

Developed in 2021 adjacent to the original, Lotus Palm offers 525 homes with pricing from $1,645,000–$3,295,000. The 65-day average market time—faster than any other GL community—indicates exceptional demand. HOA fees run approximately $679 monthly.

Lotus Edge – The New Flagship

Opening in 2024 on a 330-acre former Johns family farm (acquired for $215 million), Lotus Edge is GL Homes' most ambitious Lotus development yet. With 655-665 planned homes and what GL Homes describes as "the most impressive clubhouse we have ever built," this community is setting new standards.

  • Pricing: Fiji Collection from $1.6M, Bali Collection from $2.1M (range up to $3M)
  • Home Sizes: 1,966–6,886 sq ft
  • Clubhouse: 39,000 sq ft (largest in Lotus series)
  • Unique Amenities: Padel courts (exclusive to Lotus Edge), cold plunge pool, indoor pickleball
  • Standard Features: Sub-Zero and Wolf appliances in Bali Collection homes

Phase I amenities completed late summer 2025, with the full clubhouse opening spring 2026. Current promotions offer up to $20,000 in closing cost savings through GL Financial & Nova Title.

The Bridges: Where It All Began

The Bridges in Delray Beach (built 2012) serves as the predecessor to Seven Bridges and Boca Bridges. With 591 homes across four collections, pricing runs $1,550,000–$2,995,000 with an average sale price of $1,910,017.

The 17,000-square-foot clubhouse with "The Grille" restaurant delivers the GL Homes lifestyle at a more accessible price point. For buyers who want the Bridges experience without the Boca Bridges price tag, this community deserves serious consideration.

What This Means for Buyers

If you're considering a GL Homes community, here's my advice:

Best Value Per Square Foot: Symphony Bay ($214–343/sq ft), Saturnia Isles ($241–353/sq ft), and Valencia Falls ($220–279/sq ft) offer GL Homes quality at accessible price points.

Strongest Recent Appreciation: Saturnia Boca (+6% year-over-year) and Tuscany (97% list-to-sale ratio) show continued demand.

Newest Amenities: Lotus Edge delivers the largest clubhouse and exclusive padel courts if cutting-edge matters to you.

Fastest Absorption: If you find something you like in Lotus Palm or Tuscany, move quickly—homes don't last long.

What This Means for Sellers

If you own in a GL Homes community and are considering selling:

Seven Bridges sellers benefit from the community's 10-year appreciation track record and 75–86 day sales velocity—faster than comparable non-GL luxury homes.

Lotus community sellers should highlight contemporary design differentiation. The 94–96% list-to-sale ratios demonstrate that buyers pay near asking price for the right property.

All GL Homes sellers can leverage the "no equity membership" value proposition—buyers get country club amenities without $150,000+ initiation fees.
